Abstract

This paper examines how Apple Inc. uses rhetoric to position itself as a privacy champion in the discourse surrounding big data. Through analysis of the white paper "A Day in the Life of Your Data" and Apple's marketing campaigns, the paper reveals the rhetorical strategies—including emotional appeals, credibility appeals, and logical appeals—that Apple employs to distinguish itself from competitors and justify its data collection practices while claiming to protect user privacy.
