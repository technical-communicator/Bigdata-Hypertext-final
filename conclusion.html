Conclusion

Harking back to the definition of data and big data mentioned respectively in the first half of this essay, that data is information that can be used for calculating and reasoning and big data is data that is cloud based, real-time, and unstructured data that is sourced from decentralized entities and IOT devices (Montag and Baumeister 444). The rhetorical dimensions of big data are multifaceted and are built upon the basic structure and function of data large and small. This rhetorical analysis on the position that Apple Inc has taken on data privacy offers a great example of the duality of big data rhetoric. Apple offers a perfect example of how rhetors can craft a message that targets their audience through a variety of positions. Apple took a situation where they played a major part in the mishandling of user data and turned it on its head to rebrand as a privacy centric company set on protecting its users from the utilization of their personal data. By examining the rhetoric found in multiple artifacts from Apple Inc on their position on big data, this analysis shows how Apple positions itself to their audiences, the rhetorical situation that prompted such artifacts, the design choices that encompass the rhetoric used, the rhetorical strategies used to persuade audiences, and how the rhetoric of the artifact is situated in the larger discussion around big data.
